Private Money Lenders

Trying to find a private money loan can be tricky and difficult to do on your own. Most say they contacted private lenders in desperation, usually after banks turned them down. They know they're paying more for the money but say they have no other options. The struggle for credit has led business owners who had never ventured outside the highly regulated world of banks and credit cards to seek relationships with all kinds of other lenders. Tenant Improvements are an examples of an often difficult to finance type of project using traditional bank assistance. Used Equipment Loans are another example of where a business owner knows exactly what they need and have on-staff expertise to deal with used equipment and its challenges and are willing to manage the risk of equipment breakdown. Private money loans can be that solution to these and other situations that allow you the business owner to save money or provide a do-it-yourself aspect to your business with tenant improvement or purchasing non-qualified or used equipment or equipment for a niche business where traditional bank lending won’t work.
